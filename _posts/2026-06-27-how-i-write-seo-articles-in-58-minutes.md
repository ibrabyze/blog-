## Why most AI-written content never ranks

Most people use AI for content the same way they used to use a freelance writer with no brief — they paste in a topic and hope for the best.

The output reads fine. It says nothing specific. It covers exactly what every other page on that topic already covers. Google has no reason to rank it above the ten articles that already say the same thing in roughly the same order.

The fix is not better prompting. It is a better system — one that forces specificity at every step before a single sentence of the article gets written.

## How to write SEO articles with AI: the 58-minute system

Six steps. Fifty-eight minutes. The same sequence, every time.

1. Keyword and intent validation — 8 minutes
2. Competitor content gap analysis — 10 minutes
3. Article outline generation — 7 minutes
4. Section-by-section draft — 20 minutes
5. On-page SEO optimisation pass — 8 minutes
6. Readability and conversion edit — 5 minutes

## Step 1 — Keyword and intent validation (8 min)

Before writing anything, I confirm two things: that the keyword has real search demand, and that I understand exactly what the searcher wants when they type it.

A keyword without confirmed intent produces an article that answers the wrong question — technically about the right topic, but useless to the person reading it.

## Step 2 — Competitor content gap analysis (10 min)

I pull the top 5–8 ranking pages for the target keyword and ask: what is every single one of these missing?

This is the single highest-leverage step in the entire process. An article that fills a real gap outperforms an article that simply repeats what already exists — even if the writing quality is identical.

## Step 3 — Article outline generation (7 min)

With the gaps identified, I build a structure that addresses every one of them — in the order a reader actually needs them, not the order that feels natural to write in.

## Step 4 — Section-by-section draft (20 min)

I write in sections, not in one pass. Each section gets its own focused prompt with the outline, the gap analysis, and the specific job that section needs to do.

This single change — section-by-section instead of one long prompt — is responsible for most of the quality difference between AI content that reads generically and AI content that reads like it was written by someone who actually knows the subject.

## Step 5 — On-page SEO optimisation pass (8 min)

Title tag, meta description, header hierarchy, internal linking opportunities, keyword placement — checked and corrected in one focused pass once the draft exists.

## Step 6 — Readability and conversion edit (5 min)

The final pass removes anything that reads like AI wrote it — overly balanced sentences, unnecessary hedging, repeated sentence structures — and tightens the call to action.
